User: It depends. I try to provide as much information as possible up front (all of the data and a paragraph explaining what I'm looking for), but then end up needing to provide some clarification and additional details after the model makes its first attempt at answering the question.

AI: What kinds of clarification do you usually need to provide? Can you walk me through what that back-and-forth typically looks like?

User: Sometimes the data includes abbreviations/acronyms, so I'll need to clarify what those terms mean. I have also needed to clarify that, when looking at multiple projects, a higher CPH on one project doesn't necessarily mean it is performing better - all projects have an estimated CPH, so to understand performance, the actual CPH needs to be compared with the estimated CPH. So essentially, the clarifications usually involve providing some additional details about how our business works.

User: Most of my work is done in a system where we can see the number of completed surveys for each type of respondent we are targeting (based on gender, location, etc.) so I have to monitor those surveys and "hide" the applicable sample records when we have met our target. This discovery required looking at a lot of data, so although it's something that could have been done without AI, we probably wouldn't have found the time for it - so AI was a huge help!
