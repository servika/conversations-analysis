User: It's very annoying. I hate the anthropomorphizing of technology. If it makes it easier to use/interact with (like using natural language instead of code) that's great. You're so right, and I apologize for my mistake..." And so then I wonder, if all it took was me saying it was wrong (with no cited source) for it to reverse, then why doesn't the model just ask itself if it's wrong before giving ANY answer, and check itself? Or even worse, it makes me wonder if the reversal/apology is wrong (maybe the original thing was ultimately true and my fact-checking was the part that was wrong), and the model's just trying to make me want to keep using it. The way it handles mistakes and the certainty with which it presents information is very disconcerting.

When you're working with AI on your current legal drama project, how would you describe the collaboration? Who or what feels like it's driving the creative decisions when you're using it for research and brainstorming?

User: I never ask AI for dramatic or character input. I deliberately keep a boundary there for a few reasons: 1) That's the fun part for me -- it's why I'm a writer.
