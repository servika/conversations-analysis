Sometimes this requires special information related to their issues. I also use AI to double check things like medic math and applications related to running medications or investigating the components of those medications.

It's a great resource for education and information.

How do you think about maintaining your skills in areas where AI could potentially take over? Is this something you actively consider?

User: I don't believe AI will impact paramedicine in terms of jobs. The mechanics of ambulances and stretchers and moving people into and out of houses and hospitals are too tactile and offer too many varieties of challenges. There is also the element of liability for all of this. Also, emergency situations are messy and require speed and flexibility. I don't see AI having a huge negative impact with EMS.
If anything AI will make paramedics way better. Safety features on driving ambulances will get better (assuming full automation of driving is still at least ten years out). Making equipment and diagnostic related equipment better is an obvious advantage, especially regarding AED and Lifepack gear.
I think AI will also have the potential to help train and education EMS providers to a much higher standard using scenario software and dummies for practice.
AI will be a much worse threat in replacing doctors I suspect. Radiologists are already obsolete if not for the protection of inertia in the system.

AI: That's a thoughtful perspective - you see AI enhancing paramedic work rather than replacing it, especially given all the physical and unpredictable elements of what you do.
