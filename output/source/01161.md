User: I use AI (generally ChatGPT) in the early stages of the article process. I first ask the model the basic premise of my article, I ask it to list the top ten festivals for tourists in France or to give me 20 tips for travelling to Portugal with children or when is the best time to visit Bali and why?
I will then use this information as a jumping off point for my own research, looking up each point that the model made, fact checking it and adding more information as I find it. 

Once I have all my research done and I have done a rough first draft, of the articles body, I tend to put that draft into a chat and ask the model to create an introductory paragraph, I do this because I always struggle to  come up with the first lines of an article, and staring at a blank page is not helpful, once the model has given me a starting point the rest of the article flows much more easily. However I always rewrite that introduction myself during my edits so that no part of the article is AI written as many of my clients prohibit this. 

I may also feed the finished article into a chat and ask the model to create a except of around 60 words, an SEO-optimised tag line and a tweet linking to the article. I do this because the model will often pull out points that I had not realised were key and I can then blend the model and my own highlights to create strong links. 

I have custom preferences set for my chats so that the model knows the clents preferese tone and style and what the end goal for the article is.

Can you describe a specific recent project where you used AI in this way? What role did it play, and how did that feel for you as you moved through that process?

User: In another project for a tech comparison site I was editing a series of articles comparing very similar tech products. part of my role was to add in pros and cons for each of the products in each of the articles. I fed the almost complete articles into a chat and asked the model to come up with 3 different pros and cons for each product mentions using information from the articles and the models knowledge of the products as a whole, stressing that each one should be different from the others and the model should avoid using generic information that could apply to all of them. 
Again the preferences were set so the model knew which tone and language to use. 
I found this method sped up my editing process considerable. I had to fact check some of the information but doing so was much faster than me struggling to create 50+ individually varied pro/cons on my own.

Getting stuck because I can't think of a  name for the local sheriff who is only in one scene, or the town my characters' grandmother grew up in that is not mentioned again, can derail a productive writing session.
