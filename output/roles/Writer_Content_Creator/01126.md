Could you tell me a bit about your creative work and what a typical project looks like for you?

User: I work as a screenwriter for film and television. "Typical" varies quite a lot in my line of work. The circumstances around one project are never quite the same. However, you could probably categorise it into three buckets: original writing, rewriting, writing for hire. If I'm doing original writing, I will come up with an idea for a story, determine whether it's best suited to TV or film, then start to ideate on story, characters etc, until I have a sense of the overall plot of the feature or pilot/first season of the TV show. I'll then most likely write it on spec and have my agent send it out, rather than try pitching it out. For rewriting (usually film), I will digest the materials that need work, dissect flaws and strengths, how they can be improved, whether I am the right person to do so, and will then pitch my take on what/how to improve the project to the filmmakers involved. If I get the job, I will then try and execute this take, enhancing the script as a whole along the way, in addition to the specifics in my pitch. Usually, there will be at least one revision step where I can adjust/course-correct/inject further feedback from the filmmakers. In terms of writing for hire, I will be interviewed by show-runners, filmmakers etc. based on the strength of my previous work and then be invited to make suggestions or pitch my take on underlying material or ideas. My job will be to serve whoever is in charge of the creative vision, enabling their intentions and, if given the job, helping to execute this vision.

AI: That's a really comprehensive overview of the different types of projects you work on. It sounds like there's quite a bit of variation in terms of creative control and collaboration depending on which bucket a project falls into.
